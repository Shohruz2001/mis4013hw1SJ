Geography

Tajikistan is a landlocked country located in Central Asia, bordered by Afghanistan, China, Kyrgyzstan, and Uzbekistan. Around 93% of Tajikistan's territory is mountainous, with the Pamir Mountains offering some of the highest peaks in the world.

History

Tajikistan's history dates back thousands of years and includes periods of Persian, Russian, and Soviet rule. It became independent in 1991 after the collapse of the Soviet Union. The country faced a civil war from 1992 to 1997, which had lasting effects on its development.

Economy

Tajikistan's economy is primarily based on agriculture, aluminum production, and hydropower. The country is rich in natural resources, including precious metals and minerals. Remittances from citizens working abroad, particularly in Russia, also contribute significantly to the economy.

Tourism

Tourism in Tajikistan is growing, with visitors attracted to the country's rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes. The Pamir Highway is one of the most famous tourist routes, offering breathtaking views of the mountains and remote villages.
